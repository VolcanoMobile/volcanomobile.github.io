Android

MIDI USB Mode

Turn your Android device in a MIDI device through the Mico-USB port.

First connect your Android device to an USB device with a Micro USB to USB cable.

Then choose the MIDI mode in the Android USB Mode settings,

Your Android device can now be seen as a MIDI device

If you connected your Android device to your Computer, it should be available in the MIDI ports list in your DAW.

Samsung Android device available in qjack on Ubuntu

And new Virtual MIDI Ports are available in Applications using MIDI.

Cables

Micro USB Male to USB Male

Connect your Android device to MIDI USB Female devices

Micro USB Male to USB Male allow you to connect your Android device to your computer

It is the same cable used for charging your Android device or transfering data to your computer

After you make the connection, don't forget to setup your Android device in MIDI USB Mode

OTG

Connect your Android device to MIDI USB devices

USB OTG (USB On-The-Go) allow you to connect USB devices to your Android device through the Micro-USB port.

When a USB MIDI device is connected, it should be available in Applications using MIDI.

On the web

MIDI-USB Adapter

Connect your Android device to MIDI DIN devices

MIDI-USB Adapter allow you to connect USB device to MIDI DIN devices.

To connect it to your Android device you will also need an OTG cable.

On the web

DIN MIDI Bluetooth adapter

Connect Bluetooth MIDI Devices to your Android device

DIN MIDI Bluetooth adapter allow you to connect MIDI-DIN devices to your Android devices through MIDI Bluetooth.

Monitoring

MIDI Scope

This app is primarily intended for app developers and OEMs to test MIDI.

It displays MIDI messages as hex bytes and by name. It also displays timestamps. The app provides a MidiDeviceService that other apps can write to.
